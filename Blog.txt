The Willow Creek Observatory Incident of 2024

On October 17, 2024, a small research team at the Willow Creek Observatory conducted an overnight experiment to measure atmospheric light distortion in a remote valley near Pine Ridge, Colorado.

The team consisted of four members:

Dr. Lena Hart (Project Lead)
Omar Castillo (Systems Engineer)
Mina Zhao (Data Analyst)
Ethan Brooks (Field Technician)

The experiment began at 8:15 PM local time and ended at 3:42 AM the following morning.

The primary instrument used was the Asterion MK-3 Optical Sensor, serial number AST-MK3-88271. The sensor was mounted on Platform B7, located exactly 2.3 kilometers north of the observatory's main dome.

During the test, the team collected 18,742 individual measurements. The largest recorded distortion value was 0.873 arcseconds, observed at 11:26 PM.

A temporary power interruption occurred at 12:04 AM and lasted exactly 97 seconds. During this interruption, the backup generator labeled GEN-ALPHA-04 automatically activated and restored power.

The weather conditions remained stable throughout most of the night:

Average temperature: 6.8°C
Lowest temperature: 3.1°C
Average wind speed: 11 km/h
Humidity range: 54%–68%

At 1:17 AM, Omar Castillo discovered an anomaly in the sensor logs. A calibration value that should have remained fixed at 42.500 had unexpectedly changed to 42.537.

Further investigation revealed that a software patch deployed three days earlier, identified as Patch WC-2024.10.14-R2, had introduced a rounding error in the calibration module.

The issue affected exactly 1,284 measurements, representing approximately 6.85% of the night's collected data.

Mina Zhao created a correction script named recalibrate_v3.py to repair the affected records. The script completed processing in 4 minutes and 12 seconds.

After correction, the final dataset contained 18,742 valid measurements, with no data loss.

The team published an internal report titled:

"Analysis of Optical Distortion Variance in the Pine Ridge Valley Experiment"

The report was assigned document ID:

WCO-PRV-2024-117

The final report was approved on October 24, 2024, by Dr. Lena Hart.

As a follow-up recommendation, the observatory decided that all future calibration software would require a secondary validation process before deployment to production systems.
